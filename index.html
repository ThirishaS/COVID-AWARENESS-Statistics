Coronavirus Daily Cases and Deaths in India, Covid Vaccine India Highlights:

    Vaccination is the only way out of the coronavirus pandemic and India is struggling to say the least. Sample this – Only 3 per cent population is fully vaccinated in the nation of 133 crore! In addition, there is this confusing policy by the Narendra Modi government. Delhi Chief Minister Arvind Kejriwal has said that it is because of the Centre’s complicated policy that states are fighting with each other for the vaccine. As of now, as many as 10 states have decided to or have already floated global tenders for corona vaccines.
    From Delhi to Bengaluru, cities are shutting down the vaccine centres due to a shortage of stocks. But it should be noted that these are those people who were successfully able to register and get a Cowin slot for the Covid-19 vaccine. The situation remains abysmal in the villages. After Ghazipur, Balia, Chhapara and Buxur, now reports are coming in about floating bodies being spotted in Ganga near Patna. There are reports that locals have cast fishing nets to prevent the bodies from being disposed of in the holy Ganga.
    As the world’s second-most populous nation tries to find a way out of the second Covid wave, here are the latest, confirmed updates from India and around the globe:
    With 3,26,098 new coronavirus cases on May 15 2021, India now has registered a total of 2,43,72,907 COVID-19 infections across the country. This includes 3,890 new deaths which have taken the total count to 2,66,207. The total number of coronavirus cases also includes 2,04,32,898 people who have recovered and 36,73,802 who are currently being treated. There were 3,26,098 new active cases and 3,53,299 new recovered cases on May 15 2021. The data for this was released by the Union Health Ministry at 5:30 am
    According to the latest numbers, Maharashtra has the highest number of coronavirus cases at 53,09,215. Of this 6,01,235 are active, 47,07,980 are recovered and 79,552 have died. Karnataka and Kerala are next with 21,30,267 and 20,85,583 total COVID-19 cases respectively. Lakshadweep, Andaman and Nicobar Islands and Mizoram have the lowest number coronavirus cases with 4,568, 6,542 and 8,499 respectively.
    India currently has a fatality rate of 1.11 per cent due to coronavirus. The government has said more than 70 per cent of the people recover from COVID-19 with mild or very mild symptoms. Such cases may not even require admission to COVID-19 blocks or dedicated hospitals, the Health Ministry said.
    Across the world, there have been a total of 16,18,82,082 confirmed coronavirus cases, according to the latest update. This includes 6,07,83,205 people who are being treated, 9,77,41,777 who have recovered and 33,57,100 who have died.The highest number of coronavirus cases currently is in US which has registered 3,28,93,586 confirmed COVID-19 cases. 5,85,224 people have died of coronavirus in the country. India and Brazil are next in line with 2,43,72,907 and 1,55,19,525 confirmed coronavirus cases and 2,66,207 and 4,32,628 deaths respectively.
    In India, from 3 January 2020 to 6:18pm CEST, 14 May 2021, there have been 24,046,809 confirmed cases of COVID-19 with 262,317 deaths, reported to WHO. As of 3 May 2021, a total of 175,171,482 vaccine doses have been administered.
